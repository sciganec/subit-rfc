## **3. Agent State Model (Extension of SUBIT‑RFC)**

SUBIT‑RFC defines *Agent State* as an abstract container.  
SUBIT‑Economic formalizes it through five canonical vectors:

### **3.1. NEED (Demand Vector)**
Structural deficits preventing optimal functioning.

Includes:

- essential needs (energy, housing, safety),  
- operational needs (tools, knowledge, access),  
- social needs (support, coordination),  
- developmental needs (learning, scaling).

### **3.2. CAP (Capability Vector)**
Potential contributions:

- skills,  
- resources,  
- time,  
- cognitive capacity,  
- infrastructure access.

### **3.3. CONTR (Contribution Ledger)**
Historical record of actions that increased field coherence.

### **3.4. TRUST (Reliability Metric)**
Predictability and consistency of agent behavior.

### **3.5. FIT (Coherence Contribution Metric)**
Degree to which an agent’s actions increase or decrease global coherence.

FIT is the **primary economic signal** in SUBIT‑Economic.
