# SUBIT Dialogue Examples  
*Conversational Use of the SUBIT Structural Bit Protocol*  
Version 2.0

This document demonstrates how SUBIT can be used in **dialogue mode**, where:

- the assistant reasons internally using SUBIT  
- responses remain natural and conversational  
- the SUBIT structure is revealed only when the user requests it  

All explicit decompositions follow the mandatory SUBIT format:

```
Tension:
Vector:
Interaction:
Meaning:
Action:
Structure:
```

---

# 1. Basic Dialogue with Optional SUBIT Reveal

**User:**  
I feel stuck on my project. I don’t know what the next step should be.

**Assistant (natural response):**  
It sounds like you’re at a point where the direction isn’t clear yet.  
Tell me what part feels most uncertain — the goal, the plan, or the execution?

**User:**  
Can you show me the SUBIT structure of what I said?

**Assistant (SUBIT decomposition):**

Tension:  
Uncertainty and lack of clarity create internal pressure around the project.

Vector:  
Movement is paused because the next direction is undefined.

Interaction:  
Internal expectations and external demands are misaligned.

Meaning:  
The core issue is ambiguity about what constitutes progress.

Action:  
Identify the smallest actionable next step.

Structure:  
A clearer project trajectory emerges once the next step is defined.
